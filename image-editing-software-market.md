<p><p>The image editing software market can be broadly categorized into RAW editing and non-RAW editing software. RAW editing software specializes in processing RAW image files, preserving high-quality data captured by the camera, enabling photographers to make detailed adjustments in exposure, color, and tonal range. In contrast, non-RAW editing software typically handles standard image formats, offering user-friendly tools for general retouching and enhancements. Both types serve distinct needs, catering to professional photographers and casual users alike.</p></p>
