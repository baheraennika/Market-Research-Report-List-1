<p><p>Foot and Mouth Disease (FMD) vaccines are categorized into two primary types: emergency vaccines and conventional vaccines. Emergency vaccines are designed for rapid deployment in outbreak situations to contain and control the spread of the disease swiftly. They offer immediate protection but may require additional doses for long-term immunity. Conventional vaccines, on the other hand, are used in regular immunization programs to build herd immunity over time, ensuring sustained protection against FMD in livestock populations.</p></p>
